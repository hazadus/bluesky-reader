When you think of tuples, think of "commas", not "parentheses". ğŸ§µ

It's the commas that make a tuple, not the parentheses.

This makes a tuple:

But so does this:

This week's tips are all about tuples & tuple unpacking.

**ğŸ•’ 19:04 ğŸ‘¤ @trey.io (Trey Hunner)**
ğŸ’¬ You might wonder: do ALL commas make a tuple?

The answer is "no".

Commas in a function call separate the passed arguments:

Commas in a list literal separate the list items:

To make a list of tuples, you WOULD need parentheses:

points = [(1, 2), (3, 4)]
