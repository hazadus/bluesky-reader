**🕒 18:04 👤 @trey.io (Trey Hunner)**
💬 Python Tip #135 (of 365):

Embrace the infinite 🧵

Remember that infinite iterables are possible in Python.

itertools.count() is like an infinite range().

itertools.repeat() and itertools.cycle() are like infinite self-concatenation.

#Python #DailyPythonTip

[Открыть пост](https://bsky.app/profile/trey.io/post/3mlvw7egksn2q)

----
**🕒 18:04 👤 @trey.io (Trey Hunner)**
💬 Infinite iterables may seem like a BAD THING... but they can be handy!

You CAN limit infiniteness as needed.

By default, zip() will stop at the end of the shortest iterable... so an infinite iterable and a finite one will result in a finite iterable.

[Открыть пост](https://bsky.app/profile/trey.io/post/3mlvw7ejtps2e)

----
**🕒 18:04 👤 @trey.io (Trey Hunner)**
💬 You can always explicitly choose when to stop looping with "break" or a tool like itertools.takewhile().

And when writing iterable-accepting functions, consider whether you want to handle infinite iterables. If your function should handle arbitrary iterables, don't copy the passed-in iterable.
