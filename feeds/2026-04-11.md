**🕒 18:04 👤 @trey.io (Trey Hunner)**
💬 Python Tip #101 (of 365):

Use the resolve() method instead of the absolute() method on your pathlib.Path objects 🧵

Most of the time you reach for absolute(), you probably want resolve() instead.

Also, consider whether you should pass strict=True to resolve().

#Python #DailyPythonTip

[Открыть пост](https://bsky.app/profile/trey.io/post/3mjagdkr4py2o)

----
**🕒 18:04 👤 @trey.io (Trey Hunner)**
💬 The absolute() method doesn't resolve ".." or symlinks:

>>> Path("../.editorconfig").absolute()
PosixPath('/home/trey/Documents/../.editorconfig')

But resolve() does:

>>> Path("../.editorconfig").resolve()
PosixPath('/home/trey/.editorconfig')

[Открыть пост](https://bsky.app/profile/trey.io/post/3mjagdo2ooz2o)

----
**🕒 18:04 👤 @trey.io (Trey Hunner)**
💬 Also consider passing strict=True.

When strict=True is passed to resolve(), if the path doesn't exist or there's a symlink loop, an OSError exception is raised.

By default, the path is resolved as far as possible and any remainder is appended without checking whether it exists.
