💬 Python Tip #134 (of 365):

Use looping helpers 🧵

Python's "for" loops are simple: they loop over an iterable one item at a time.

That's all they can do.

Because our "for" loops are so simple and iterable-centric, looping helpers are a VERY big deal in Python.

#Python #DailyPythonTip

[Открыть пост](https://bsky.app/profile/trey.io/post/3mltfqkocpm2i)

----
**🕒 18:04 👤 @trey.io (Trey Hunner)**
💬 We use enumerate to count upward while looping, reversed to loop in the reverse direction, and zip to loop over multiple iterables at the same time.

When looping, think in terms of looping helpers.

What tool, if it existed, could enhance your "for" loop?

**🕒 18:04 👤 @trey.io (Trey Hunner)**
💬 Python has those 3 built-in looping helpers (enumerate, reversed, zip), but there are many other looping helpers that live in the itertools module.

And there are looping helpers hiding in third-party libraries... plus you can always write your own with generator functions or generator expressions.
