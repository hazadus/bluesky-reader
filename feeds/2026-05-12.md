**🕒 18:04 👤 @trey.io (Trey Hunner)**
💬 Python Tip #132 (of 365):

Know your iterator concepts. 🧵

Items are "consumed" from an iterator as you loop over it.

And iterators can be "exhausted" if their items have all been consumed.

But not all iterators CAN be exhausted. Infinitely long iterators do exist.

#Python #DailyPythonTip

[Открыть пост](https://bsky.app/profile/trey.io/post/3mloes4fdkq2v)

----
**🕒 18:04 👤 @trey.io (Trey Hunner)**
💬 You can get the next iterator item, but not the previous one.

Also, there's no way to look at the next item in an iterator without consuming that item.

Iterators have no cache and no reset button. Well they CAN, but it's unusual. File objects I think of: they're iterators but seek(0) resets them.
